BMW 1 Series M

Modeling a sport car is part of every CG artist's rite of passage. It requires a great mix of technical modelling skills and an eye for lighting. Plus, the photorealistic pathtracing renderer Cycles was just implemented in Blender, I had to see what it can do.

Instead of going with the usual choices of Italian marques, I ended up modelling something that I am more passionable about: a rather 'old-school' BMW. It doesn't have the sleekness of Ferrari, nor the muscularity of Lamborghini, but the bullfrog 1-Series M has undeniable presence.


Getting good photo references took a while because the car was just released. The acutal modelling process was pretty straight forward using subdivision surfaces.




For the car paint material, I was able to duplicate the valencia orange with a clearcoat lacqure. But I wasn't able to duplicate the metalic flakes in the paint to my satisfaction. So for now, solid orange it is.
